* Review status and authority

This is a non-normative blocking impact review. It records a conflict and its
admission consequences; it does not revise the time API, amend a requirement,
invalidate evidence by itself, or authorize implementation. The active design,
registered amendments, work-package DAG, admission audit, and evidence records
must be updated and independently reviewed before the affected work proceeds.

* Executive verdict

The proposed =clinkz_wot_core::Deadline= is not implementation-ready. The
design describes modular comparison for finite clocks, while the implemented
foundation instant supports only non-wrapping checked comparison and the
proposed Deadline carries no wrap policy. The conflict also reaches existing
=CleanupRecord= timing validation and the completed WP-000
=time-and-generation-api= evidence.

=Deadline= and =WP-100-HANDLER-ENTRY= are blocked by the registered
=TIME-DOMAIN-AND-DEADLINE= impact scope. That record is a structured placeholder,
not an admitted or fully defined corrective tranche: the future tranche's
identity, ownership, dependencies, completion contract, and evidence disposition
remain unfrozen. The other five passive values from the candidate are disjoint
from this finding and may be reviewed as a separately projected five-value
scope. This review alone does not admit that scope.

* Blocking conflict

For one clock with raw period 256, a deadline stored at tick 250 and a later
observation at tick 3 cross a hardware wrap. =MonotonicInstant::checked_cmp=
performs ordinary =u64= ordering and documents that it applies to non-wrapping
instants. =Deadline::checked_is_elapsed_at= receives only the stored instant and
the current instant, so it has no period or epoch with which to recover the
ordering.

The half-period duration rule does not close the issue. A constrained caller
may delay manual progress for more than half a period after expiry. A stateless
modular comparison then cannot distinguish an observation before the deadline
from a late observation after one or more wraps.

The existing cleanup path has the same exposure: =CleanupRecord::try_with_timing=
checks clock identities and then compares =retry_not_before.ticks()= directly
with =deadline.ticks()=. Its ordering is correct only if equal-clock tick values
belong to a non-wrapping logical domain.

* Evidence impact

The completed WP-000 =time-and-generation-api= record covers =TIME-001= and
claims finite-clock wrap declaration. It demonstrates that a clock can report a
period, but it does not demonstrate correct cross-wrap ordering, delayed polling,
deadline expiry, source freshness, or cleanup retry ordering.

That evidence's time claim requires explicit impact disposition and replacement
or reaffirmation after the clock model is frozen. Review 05's statement that
completed foundation evidence need not be reopened remains valid for the
disjoint resource/profile and linear-budget refresh, but not for this newly
identified WP-000 time evidence gap.

Existing cleanup timing evidence is also impacted to the extent that it assumes
raw equal-clock tick comparison. Cleanup identity, retry-count limits,
redaction, and bounded-record evidence are otherwise outside this finding.

* Five-value disjointness

The clock-independent candidate consists exactly of:

- =CancellationView=;
- =SubscriptionAcceptance=;
- =HandlerFootprint=;
- =HandlerStep<R>=; and
- =StaticHandlerRegistration<'h, H>=.

None contains a =ClockId=, =MonotonicInstant=, duration, deadline, source
timestamp, wrap policy, timeout transition, or clock-driven state. =HandlerStep=
uses a work budget rather than elapsed time, and =CancellationView= is a passive
snapshot rather than the authoritative cancellation/deadline owner. Their
derive, ownership, const-construction, resource-declaration, and redacted-Debug
contracts can therefore be reviewed without choosing a clock model.

This disjointness requires =Deadline= and =TIME-001= to remain absent from the
candidate's authoritative tranche, audit, checker, implementation paths, and
completion evidence. Any reintroduction reopens this finding and revokes that
candidate's admission.

* Recommended disposition

Apply two separate actions:

1. isolate =Deadline= immediately and re-review the other five values as an
   exact, independently admitted tranche; and
2. retain a structured blocking-scope record spanning the known foundation and
   Core impacts, then define and independently admit corrective work only after
   its identity, ownership, dependencies, completion contract, and evidence
   disposition are frozen.

The preferred long-term direction is for =RuntimeClock::now()= to return
extended, non-wrapping logical ticks for one stable =ClockId=. The clock adapter,
not each Deadline or consumer, owns raw-counter epoch extension. A reset or lost
epoch must not reuse the old clock id. =SourceTimestamp::Monotonic= follows the
same rule. =wrap_period_ticks= is then deprecated or retained only as raw-source
diagnostic metadata and never participates in instant ordering.

Carrying a modular policy in each Deadline is not recommended. It duplicates
clock metadata across consumers, expands SourceTimestamp and cleanup contracts,
and remains ambiguous after an unobserved half period unless epoch state is
introduced anyway.

* Conditions to clear the finding

The blocking finding remains open until:

- the authoritative time model selects and freezes one comparison domain;
- =TIME-001= and the handler amendment have consistent ownership and affected-
  requirement projections;
- the =None= result of an incomparable deadline has an exact error category,
  phase, retry class, and dispatcher behavior;
- a future corrective tranche or other authorized work record freezes its
  identity, ownership, dependencies, affected prior-evidence disposition,
  checks, and completion keys;
- no-std fixtures prove raw-wrap extension and delayed-poll behavior;
- SourceTimestamp and CleanupRecord tests prove the selected semantics; and
- WP-000 time evidence is explicitly replaced or reaffirmed.

* Verdict

WP-000 time evidence: IMPACTED; disposition required.

Deadline value: FAIL; blocked.

Broad handler entry: FAIL; blocked.

Clock-independent five-value scope: DISJOINT, but not admitted by this review.
